Welcome to the SketchBug Study

Hello! Thank you for joining us today.

Before we begin, I’d like to give you a quick overview of what this study is about.

We’re exploring the potential of using sketching and hand-drawn gestures as a way to interact with a debugger.

Instead of focusing on which tool is better, we’re more interested in how people naturally interact with sketch-based debugging tools, what they find intuitive, where it breaks down, and what kinds of problems it might be well suited for.

During this session, you’ll try out two tools we’ve developed:

  • A sketch-based debugger that allows you to interact with code using pen-like gestures
  • A baseline interface modeled after the Visual Studio Code interactive debugger, built by us for this study

You’ll receive a short tutorial for each tool, have a chance to explore it freely, and then complete a set of tasks.

We’ll also ask you to speak aloud as you work, and we may follow up with a few reflection questions.

The whole study will take about 45 to 60 minutes. Your screen will be recorded for analysis.

If you’re ready, we’ll get started!
